We Are SCENe

Student Cultural Exchange Network (SCENe) is an organisation focusing on integrating local and non-local students on campus. Founded by diverse students within the HKU (The University of Hong Kong) community, SCENe is proudly supported by The University of Hong Kong.

Inclusivity

is at the core of SCENe events. We foster an inclusive and respectful environment by creating diverse events that enable anyone and everyone to participate if they so desire. The SCENe team emphasizes inclusivity. Anyone and everyone can join SCENe without any membership fees or selection criteria.

Individuality

is SCENe’s signature. Each event positively impacts the HKU community and Hong Kong in general. Every event uniquely caters to people of different cultures, backgrounds, and interest.

Sustainability

is rooted in SCENe’s ideology. We consistently strive to be environmentally friendly in order to reduce our carbon footprint. This ensures that future generations will still be able to enjoy the many fruits Mother Nature provides.

Our Events

  • HK-12

    SCENe brought our version of The Amazing Race to HKU in April 2015, with 12 teams competing in a race across Hong Kong for a grand prize of $3500. Our primary goal, as always, was to integrate students from different walks of life, while providing them with a new way to experience Hong Kong.

  • Curse of Camp Tung Lung Chau

    Halloween 2015 was a different experience for those who attended our camping event. With tents, sleeping bags and food provided by SCENe, “The Curse of Camp Tung Lung Chau” featured BBQ, panoramic views from one of Hong Kong’s beautiful outlying islands, and spooky stories told around the campfire.

  • Holi 2015

    Holi 2015 was our second time celebrating the Hindu holiday of colours. Colourful coloured powder, mouth watering traditional indian food and Bollywood music were provided to participants.

  • Tastes of the World

    A celebration of the foods from around the world. Delicious snacks and meals from every single continent were served in abundance and if that wasn't entertainment enough, we had musical and dance performances from highly talented HKU students.

  • Holi 2014

    Holi is a festival from India that is celebrated all over the world and is famous for its use of brightly coloured powders. SCENe was proud to co-host this beautiful festival of spring with the South Asian Society, and introduce it to people from different cultures and countries.

  • Monday Mixer

    Monday Mixer was a unique event where participants were put in groups and given games to help them converse with each other. Groups were made at random, which made it easy to find strangers.

Our Team

Each SCENE committee member is carefully chosen to represent the diverse varieties of cultural, religious, and geographical backgrounds present at HKU. Our committee members have represented over 10 different countries, 7 different HKU faculties, and collectively speak over 14 different languages. Each team member’s opinion is equally valued and respected, and their ideas represent our promise to make our events a unique experience for HKU students.

FAQs

What's up with the little e in your name?

We could have stuck with SCEN, but we didn't know how to pronounce it, and we realized that the word 'Network' had a little e after the N, which completes the word SCENe. So we added the e.

Why do you care about integration?

University is a great place to learn from the unique perspectives of people from all over the world. A well integrated student body will mean a more worldly student body.

Can’t HKU students integrate themselves?

No, unfortunately. This is not a problem unique to HKU and isn’t anyone’s fault. There are language barriers hindering communication and groups that people are hesitant to come out of.

How do your events work?

Our events promote cultures from around the world. They are fun and challenging which creates a comfortable atmosphere for people to mingle without the need for verbal communication.

How do your events help?

In our events, we create little pushes for participants to mingle. In HK-12, for example, we created the most diverse teams possible with the list of participants that we had.

How do I learn about future events?

We are always updating our Facebook Page and Instagram Page, you can like us there or you can join our mailing list, just input your email address at the bottom of this page.
